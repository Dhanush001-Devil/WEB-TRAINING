1.FORD MUSTANG GT

The Ford Mustang GT is a performance-focused, mid-size coupe and convertible that is part of the Mustang family. It is known for its powerful V8 engine, typically a 5.0-liter unit, and comes with a choice of manual or automatic transmissions. The Mustang GT offers a blend of American muscle car styling with modern performance features.







2.PANDA TURENO 86

The Toyota AE86 isn't totally uncommon here in America. You still see a few old of these old Corollas at car shows and drift meets here, same as you do in other foreign markets. But what you don't realize is Japan got probably something like TEN TIMES AS MANY of these things as the rest of the world.







3.DODGE CHALLANGER

The Dodge Challenger is the name of three generations of automobiles produced by the American automobile manufacturer Dodge. However, the first use of the Challenger name by Dodge dates back to 1959 for marketing a "value version" of the full-sized Coronet Silver Challenger.







4.NISSIAN SKYLINE GTR

The Nissan GT-R, known as Skyline GT-R in some markets, is a high-performance sports car produced by Nissan. It's a legendary vehicle with a powerful engine, all-wheel-drive system, and a reputation for exceptional performance. The latest GT-R model is the R35, which has been in production since 2009.







5.MAHINDRA THAR

The Mahindra Thar is a compact, four-wheel drive, off-road two-door SUV manufactured by Indian automaker Mahindra and Mahindra Ltd. It was introduced on October 4, 2010 and replaced the Mahindra Major.







6.CHEVROLET CAMARO

Bumblebee's car is a Chevrolet Camaro. Specifically, Bumblebee is typically depicted as a modified Chevrolet Camaro. The specific model and year of the Camaro have varied across different Transformers media, with appearances ranging from a 1977 Camaro to a 2007 Camaro and even a 6th-generation concept. In the 2018 film "Bumblebee," he is also seen as a 1941-45 military style Willys MB before becoming a Volkswagen Beetle.
